BBQ Scraper

Design

Step 2: Design

I used characteristics and parts that I liked from a few different scrapers that I researched in my final design. To create it, I used Sketchup for Education, a free online tool. It's very useful for this kind of thing, and it was pretty easy to learn the basics. Due to the specific curves and edges, I had to use many guide lines. I also drew a top-down view with dimensions on paper, to scale. (Both below in image carousel)

My design was pretty 'normal' and similar to most barbecue scrapers. It included a long but shallow inside curve on the neck, mainly because I liked the way it looked, but I figured it'd also be comfortable. There are a few outside curves on the handle at the top, as I wanted a pretty rounded shape for both comfort and looks. I made transition from the from the neck to the blade pretty gradual and curved, as I preferred this look from my research. There is also a hole in the handle for a string or something else to attach. The blade starts going in around halfway past the start of the wide blade part, and it goes in only from one side rather than having both sides converge in the middle. I chose this both because it looks good and I felt a slightly bulkier blade would be beneficial. I considered adding some sort of design on the handle (like the loon example from my research) or adding grooves on the handle for your fingers, but I ultimately decided against both; the former due to functionality and comfort, and the second mostly because of time and work restrictions at the time.
