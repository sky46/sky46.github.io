Safety

Woodshop General and Machine Rules

  1. Operate machines ONLY with the instructor's permission and after you have received proper instruction.
  2. Remove jewelry, eliminate loose clothing, hoods and drawstrings, ear buds/head phones and confine long hair when working in a shop environment.
  3. Always use appropriate Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) while in the shop when working with power tools. Always wear safety glasses, or a face shield.
  4. All accidents must be reported to the instructor immediately.
  5. Don’t use tools or equipment that are in any way defective/damaged. Tell the instructor immediately if the equipment doesn’t look proper.
  6. Damp, oily rags are a fire hazard. Place rags in the RED metal fireproof container in the Stain Room.
  7. Always use a hand brush to wipe away sawdust or scraps. Never use your hand.
  8. The floor must be kept clean. Don’t leave anything lying around that could be tripped on or slipped on.
  9. Carry sharp or pointed tools with the edge or point held in your hand. This will help prevent injury to others if someone bumps you or you trip.
  10. Use ALL recommended guards and safety devices on the machinery as shown by your
  11. instructor.
  12. Always unplug a tool or disconnect the circuit breaker to prevent accidental starting of the equipment when you are making changes to it.
  13. Wait for a machine to come to a COMPLETE stop before leaving it.
  14. No talking to others while operating a machine or talking to the operator.

  1. Always use appropriate eye protection.
  2. Don’t use the abrasive disc/belt/spindle if it is torn or damaged. Report it to the instructor if it is damaged.
  3. The material MUST be held flat against the table and hands kept clear of the abrasive disc/belt/spindle.
  4. ALL adjustments to the machine are made BEFORE starting it.
  5. Always check to make sure the table is adjusted for the angle that you want. Use a square to do this.
  6. Work must be done on the side of the disc rotating downward. The LEFT side
  7. On the spindle sander, sand against the direction the spindle is spinning.
  8. Do not leave the machine until it has come to a full stop.
  9. Don’t use metal on the disc, belt sander or spindle sander. Sparks can get into the dust extraction and start a fire.
  10. One operator on a disc/belt sander combination at a time.

  1. Eye protection must be worn while operating the Scroll Saw.
  2. Complete attention must be directed toward work being done, NO TALKING.
  3. Keep your fingers clear of the blade.
  4. Make ALL adjustments with the power off.
  5. NEVER clear the table with your hands, use a piece of scrap wood or a brush.
  6. Lower the HOLD-DOWN so that it presses lightly on the material being cut before starting the saw.
  7. The thicker the wood, the faster the speed of the blade.
  8. DO NOT saw stock that has an uneven surface.
  9. Occasionally the blade will break while the machine is running. If this should occur, shut off the machine and notify the instructor immediately.
  10. Hold the material firmly and feed it into the blade at a moderate speed.
  11. Before backing out of a long cut, turn the power off.
  12. Disconnect the machine from the power source when making repairs or changing blades.

  1. Eye protection must be worn while operating the Band Saw.
  2. Complete attention must be directed toward work being done, NO TALKING.
  3. Position hands on the material being cut so they are NOT IN FRONT of the blade.
  4. Fingers should never be closer than 50 mm (2 inches) to the blade.
  5. NEVER clear the table with your hands, use a piece of scrap wood or a brush.
  6. Lower the blade guard to the table after you have finished making the cuts and the blade has come to a complete stop.
  7. Make all adjustments to the machine before you start the machine. Adjust the top blade guide approximately 6 mm (1/4 inch) above the material to be cut BEFORE starting the saw.
  8. Cutting Round Stock can be dangerous on the Band Saw because it may spin when it contacts the blade. Use the special Doweling jig for this operation.
  9. Make sure the blade has come to a FULL STOP before leaving the Band saw.
  10. Occasionally the saw blade will break while the machine is in use. If this should occur, shut off the machine and notify the instructor immediately.
  11. Never back out of a long or curved cut, turn the power off.
  12. Use RELIEF CUTS when cutting sharp curves or irregular shapes.
  13. Make all cuts on the waste-side of the line
  14. Do NOT stand to the right side of the band saw. If the blade breaks it could hit you.

  1. Eye protection must be worn while operating the Drill Press.
  2. Long hair can be an extreme hazard on the Drill Press. Tie it BACK.
  3. Secure the drill bit in the chuck by placing the chuck key in one of the three holes and twisting firmly.
  4. Remove the chuck key immediately after installing, or removing a drill bit.
  5. Start drilling a hole slowly, and finish slowly, for a neat hole and to prevent chipping.
  6. Brush the table with a shop brush, NEVER with your hand.
  7. Use a SCRAP piece of plywood under the work piece to prevent damage to the Drill Press table and for a better- finished cut.
  8. To prevent injury to your hands when working on the Drill Press, SECURE YOUR WORK PIECE to the table, especially with small pieces or large diameter holes
  9. Make ALL set-up adjustments such as table height, depth stop and fastening material to the table BEFORE the machine is turned on.
  10. When drilling DEEP holes, withdraw the drill bit frequently to prevent burning and to prevent damaging the drill bit.
  11. Locate the centre of a hole to be drilled with an awl.
  12. Keep the long part of the wood to the left of the table. This is a safety measure to ensure that if the wood spins it hits the back pole.

  1. Eye protection must be worn while operating the Mitre Saw.
  2. Always use your right hand on theTRIGGER when cutting wood on theMitre Saw.
  3. NEVER place your hands near the sawblade, whether the saw is running orstopped. DO NOT cross your arms whencutting on the Mitre Saw or reaching formaterial.
  4. When cutting, keep one hand on thehandle and the other hand off the table.
  5. Always push the saw AWAY from youwhen you are slide cutting.
  6. Cut SLOWLY, especially on wide or thickpieces. Fast cuts leave a POOR finish and can jam the blade.
  7. ALWAYS check a 90-degree cut with a Square before your final cut.
  8. The Mitre Saw is for CROSSCUTTING only.
  9. Use a 'stop' when cutting several pieces the same length.
  10. Keep the saw and work area clear of extra wood, chips, sawdust and tools for safe andaccurate work. Throw all trimmings from the Mitre Saw in the garbage can, and cleanthe machine after you have finished.
  11. Always secure the wood against the fence with your hand or a clamp if it is a smallpiece and in the NO HANDS ZONE.
  12. If the wood is warped, the BOW goes upthe CUP goes down.

  1. Eye protection must be worn while operating the Jointer
  2. 305 mm (12 inches) is the shortestboard to be cut on the Jointer.
  3. Inspect the wood to be jointed. Makesure it has NO large cracks, looseknots, nails, glue, sand, paint, or anyother material that could damage thecutting blades.
  4. Joint ONLY ONE SIDE and ONEEDGE of a board. DO NOT joint theEND of the board.
  5. The amount the Jointer cuts on eachpass can be adjusted. Have the instructor adjust the INFEED table for you if it is takingtoo little or too much off.
  6. NEVER adjust the OUTFEED table setting.
  7. Make sure the fence is 90 degrees to the table using a square and all locks are secure beforeturning the Jointer on.
  8. ALWAYS joint a board in the direction of the grain.
  9. Keep the board firmly in contact with the fence and the table of the Jointer.
  10. NEVER let your fingers PASS DIRECTLY OVER the revolving cutter head.
  11. ALWAYS use an appropriate push stick when jointing a board that is below the level ofthe top of the fence.
  12. NEVER remove the safety guard from the cutter head.
  13. Position yourself parallel to your piece of wood so you will not be injured in caseof kickback and so you are balanced.
  14. Make as many passes as necessary to make the board flat.

  1. Eye protection must be worn while operating the Thickness Planer.
  2. Ear protection should be worn while operating the Thickness Planer.
  3. The SHORTEST board to be planed is 305 mm(12 inches).
  4. Set the 'depth of cut' on the Planer by the wood touching the feed rolls. Lower the table until the wood passes through the planer, thenraise the table one turn at a time until the wood no longer passes through.
  5. Inspect the wood to be planed. Make sure it has NO large cracks, loose knots, nails, glue,sand, paint, or any other material that could damage the cutting blades.
  6. When planing several boards, plane ALL boards at each thickness setting.
  7. JOINTED surface of a board always goes DOWN when planing.
  8. The wood is always planed with the grain, NEVER across the grain.
  9. Never look into the Planer while the machine is running
  10. If the wood jams or fails to feed into the Planer, shut the machine off and call the instructor.
  11. NEVER place your hands near the feed rolls or inside the Planer as it is very dangerous.
  12. 'Walk' boards hand over hand instead of sliding them through your hands to prevent slivers.
  13. Use a backing board when a piece of wood is ¼ of an inch or under in thickness.

  1. Eye protection must be worn while operating the Table Saw.
  2. All guards must be in place when operating the Table Saw. The only exception is with the instructor’s permission.
  3. Give the saw all of your attention when operating it, NO TALKING
  4. All adjustments to the fence and blade are to be made when the saw blade is stopped. You must lockout the power when changing the blade.
  5. The saw blade should be adjusted above the wood ONE TOOTH and NO MORE.
  6. Always use the fence or mitre box, freehand cuts are NOT ALLOWED on the Table Saw.
  7. NEVER stand in line with the blade or your work piece in case of a KICKBACK.
  8. Never reach across the blade to retrieve a piece of material you have cut.
  9. 305 mm (12 inches) is the minimum length for ripping.
  10. Never rip something that is wider than it is long. Use a mitre gauge or sled for this.
  11. Always use a PUSH STICK with material when ripping stock under 6” wide.
  12. The edge of the material touching the rip fence must be straight and smooth. (Face edge).
  13. Always place the part of the material to be kept, BETWEEN the fence and the blade.
  14. Completely push material being ripped past the blade and splitter. Material should never be left between the fence and the blade.
  15. Use the MITRE GAUGE, SLED, or SLIDING TABLE to guide the material when crosscutting.
  16. If crosscutting, ALWAYS check a mitre gauge with a square before cutting.
  17. The rip fence must be clear of the work area when cross cutting.
  18. NEVER use the rip fence and mitre gauge or sled at the same time.
  19. All work must be firmly held against the mitre gauge or sled.
  20. Never remove scrap material from around the saw while it is running.

  1. Eye protection must be worn while operating the Router. A face shield is to be used when using a Router freehand.
  2. Ear protection should be worn while operating the Router.
  3. ONLY plug in the Router after you have made any adjustments and you are ready to use it. Check to make sure the power switch is 'off' before you plug it in.
  4. Check the bit to make sure it is sharp and in good condition. A dull or damaged bit can be very dangerous to use.
  5. When you handle the bits, try to keep your fingers away from the sharp cutting edges.
  6. Don't try to remove a lot of material in one cut. Make several passes.
  7. The area of the stock that is going to be routed has to be in good condition. It has to be clean, with no knots, splits or checks and free of paint or hardware.
  8. The direction of feed should always be AGAINST the rotation of the bit, or it will RUN ON YOU.
  9. Plan the cut so you can stay balanced. Don't over-reach.
  10. Don’t turn off the router until the bit is clear of the stock.
  11. If using the router table keep your hands far away from the rotating bit.
  12. If using the router, make sure the fence is secure if it has one.
  13. Keep the cut steady and even. Don’t force it too fast or you can damage your material (splinter).

  1. Have the instructor check the set up before using the Lathe.
  2. A Face Shield must be used while operating the lathe.
  3. Tie back LONG HAIR, remove loose clothing and jewelry when operating the Wood Lathe. Not doing so is extremely dangerous.
  4. Check and make sure the TAIL STOCK is locked securely in place.
  5. Follow gluing instructions and allow all glued work to fully dry before turning.
  6. Check wood to be turned. DO NOT turn pieces with large cracks or large knots.
  7. After mounting rough stock on the lathe, turn the piece by hand to see that it will clear the tool rest.
  8. Start the lathe at the slowest speed then increase to the desired speed. NEVER turn large objects at high speed.
  9. Hold all Lathe tools with both hands and a firm grip.
  10. Keep lathe tools sharp, and report to the instructor when they become dull.
  11. ALWAYS stop the lathe to make measurements with calipers.
  12. Keep the tool rest as close to the work as possible, and NEVER adjust the tool rest while the lathe is turning.
  13. When sanding and finishing on the lathe, ALWAYS remove the tool rest.
  14. Use a SMALL CLOTH ONLY when finishing; fold it into a small pad to apply finishes on the lathe. NEVER WRAP THE CLOTHE AROUND YOUR HAND.
  15. Check frequently to make sure that the wood does not become loose while in the wood lathe.
