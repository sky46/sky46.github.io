Wood Joinery Frame

Design

Step 2: Design

As with the previous project, we created our designs in SketchUp for Education. This time, we just followed along as Mr. Doherty demonstrated how to make the design that we were all using. It wasn’t really much harder than the BBQ scraper, though I guess it was more 3-dimensional and had some more variety. Similarly to the last time, we had to use lots of guide lines to make the dimensions and shapes precise. However, this time, unlike with the BBQ scraper, we didn’t have to draw the design on paper.

There are 5 different joints used in the frame, which we numbered 1 to 6 (there are 2 dovetails):

  1. Mitre joint
  2. Butt joint
  3. Dovetail joint 1
  4. Dovetail joint 2
  5. Mortise-and-tenon joint
  6. Half lap joint
